Brief: 

Bookshelf Reading Pace Tracker

Build a single-page app that helps readers figure out if they'll finish a book in time for their deadline.

The user enters:
- The total number of pages in the book
- How many pages they've already read
- Their target finish date

The app instantly calculates and displays:
- Pages remaining
- Days remaining until the deadline
- Required daily reading pace (pages per day) to finish on time
- A simple visual progress bar showing how far through the book they are

If the user is already ahead of pace (they've read more than needed so far based on elapsed days since a start date they can optionally enter), highlight this with a celebratory green indicator. If they're behind, show a gentle warning in amber. The layout should feel like a cozy reading nook — warm tones, a clean card-based design, and a small bookshelf or book emoji motif. All state lives in the page; no backend required.

how the final score works

One AI judge scores all three categories from your public repository. Each category lands on a 0–100 scale, and your final score is the average of the three. Ranking, on a tie, breaks by the strongest single category, then the earliest verified commit - never by chance.

Scoring reads your code, not a screenshot. A deployed live URL is optional and never required to earn points.
tiebreaks

Final score is the average of the three category scores. If two builds tie on final score, we break it - in order, never by chance:

    1.Strongest category - the higher of your three category scores wins.
    2.Earliest verified commit - if still tied, whoever locked in their scored commit first wins. We use the time our system first saw the commit (not the commit's own timestamp), so it can't be gamed.
